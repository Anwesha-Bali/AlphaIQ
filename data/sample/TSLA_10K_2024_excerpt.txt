ITEM 1. BUSINESS

Tesla, Inc. ("Tesla", "the Company") designs, develops, manufactures,
sells, and leases high-performance fully electric vehicles and energy
generation and storage systems, and offers services related to its
products. The Company operates in two reportable segments: Automotive
and Energy generation and storage.

In fiscal 2024, total revenue was approximately $97 billion, broadly
flat versus the prior year. Automotive revenue declined low-single-
digit percent on lower average selling prices, while Energy generation
and storage revenue grew over 60 percent year over year, driven by
Megapack deployments.

ITEM 1A. RISK FACTORS

The Company operates in a highly competitive automotive market. Legacy
automakers and new entrants, particularly in China, have accelerated
electric vehicle programs. Pricing pressure has compressed automotive
gross margin throughout the year.

The Company's growth strategy depends substantially on the development
and deployment of autonomy technology, including Full Self-Driving and
robotaxi services. There is uncertainty about timelines, regulatory
approval, and ultimate performance of these technologies. Delays or
adverse outcomes could materially impact long-term valuation
assumptions.

Concentration risk arises from the Company's dependence on a small
number of vehicle programs and from manufacturing operations
concentrated in a limited number of facilities. Disruption — whether
from labor, supply chain, fire, or natural disaster — could
significantly impact production and deliveries.

Key person risk: the Company's operations and strategic direction are
substantially dependent on its Chief Executive Officer, whose
attention is divided among multiple businesses.

Regulatory risk: vehicle safety investigations, including ongoing
inquiries by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration into
driver-assist features, could result in recalls, fines, or restrictions
on product features.

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS

Automotive gross margin (excluding regulatory credits) was
approximately 17 percent for the year, a decline of roughly 300 basis
points versus the prior year. The compression reflects lower average
selling prices, partially offset by lower input costs and improved
manufacturing efficiency at newer factories.

Energy generation and storage gross margin expanded meaningfully on
higher Megapack volumes and stronger pricing.

R&D spending grew approximately 19 percent, reflecting accelerated
investment in next-generation vehicle platforms, autonomy, and
artificial intelligence training infrastructure.

Management has stated that vehicle volume growth in 2025 will depend
on the launch cadence of new, lower-cost vehicle platforms, and that
energy storage is expected to remain the fastest-growing business
segment.
