ITEM 1. BUSINESS

Apple Inc. ("Apple", "the Company") designs, manufactures, and markets
smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables, and accessories,
and sells a variety of related services. The Company's products
include iPhone, Mac, iPad, and Wearables, Home and Accessories.
Services include advertising, AppleCare, cloud, digital content,
payment, and other services.

In fiscal 2024, total net sales were approximately $391 billion,
representing year-over-year growth of approximately 2 percent.
Services revenue grew 13 percent and reached a record high, while
iPhone revenue was roughly flat. Wearables, Home and Accessories
declined modestly due to a challenging compare period.

ITEM 1A. RISK FACTORS

Macroeconomic conditions, including inflation, recession, foreign
currency volatility, and shifts in consumer spending, could materially
adversely affect the Company's business and results of operations.

Global and regional economic conditions, including persistently
elevated interest rates and ongoing geopolitical tensions, have
impacted and could continue to impact demand for the Company's
products and services.

The Company faces substantial competition in markets characterized by
rapid technological change. Competitors include companies with
comparable or greater scale, resources, and brand recognition.

Single-supplier and limited-source component dependencies create
supply concentration risk. Disruption at a key supplier or in a key
geography could impair the Company's ability to manufacture and ship
products on schedule.

The Company is subject to evolving regulatory frameworks, including
antitrust scrutiny of its App Store practices in the European Union
and in the United States, and ongoing privacy and data protection
regulation. Adverse regulatory outcomes could require changes to
business models or result in fines and other penalties.

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS

Gross margin for the year was 46.2 percent, an increase of
approximately 130 basis points compared to the prior year. The
improvement was driven primarily by higher Services mix and favorable
cost structure on iPhone, partially offset by adverse foreign exchange
movements.

Operating expenses were $57.5 billion, up 6 percent year over year,
reflecting continued investment in research and development including
generative AI capabilities and silicon engineering. R&D spending grew
9 percent.

The Company returned $110 billion to shareholders during fiscal 2024
through share repurchases and dividends, and the Board authorized an
additional $100 billion repurchase program.

Management expects services revenue to continue growing at a double-
digit pace in fiscal 2025, with iPhone unit demand being a function
of new device cycle reception and the rollout of Apple Intelligence
features to additional markets.
